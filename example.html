  1. Which should example where your equal grew.
  2. But he that writes of you, if he can tell
  3. That you are you, so dignifies his story,
  4. Not making worse what nature made so clear,
  5. Who is it that says most? which can say more
  6. In whose confine immured is the store
  7. Being fond on praise, which makes your praises worse.
  8. Let him but copy what in you is writ,
  9. You to your beauteous blessings add a curse,
  10. And such a counterpart shall fame his wit,
  11. That to his subject lends not some small glory;
  12. Than this rich praise, that you alone are you?
  13. Lean penury within that pen doth dwell
  14. Making his style admired every where.

sonnets.stanza51
  1. From where thou art why should I haste me thence?
  2. Towards thee I'll run, and give him leave to go.
  3. Thus can my love excuse the slow offence
  4. When swift extremity can seem but slow?
  5. Then should I spur, though mounted on the wind;
  6. Till I return, of posting is no need.
  7. But love, for love, thus shall excuse my jade;
  8. Since from thee going he went wilful-slow,
  9. Of my dull bearer when from thee I speed:
  10. O, what excuse will my poor beast then find,
  11. Therefore desire of perfect'st love being made,
  12. Shall neigh--no dull flesh--in his fiery race;
  13. In winged speed no motion shall I know:
  14. Then can no horse with my desire keep pace;
